Regulatory Framework: Regulated Developments

Chicago's 2024 regulations apply to most mid- and large-scale developments and redevelopments.

  • Land disturbance area: ≥ 15,000 sq. ft.
  • New contiguous impervious area: ≥ 7,500 sq. ft.
  • Sites with direct discharge to waters (e.g., Lake Michigan, Chicago River) are also regulated.

Regulated projects must comply with **two primary performance standards**: Volume Control Rate Control

Stormwater Retention (Volume Control)

+21

Goal

Reduce the total stormwater volume entering the sewer system by capturing runoff on-site.

  • Standard Requirement: Capture the first 0.5 inches of runoff from all impervious surfaces with approved BMPs.
  • Alternative Path: Provide at least a 15% reduction in total impervious area compared to existing conditions.
  • Green roofs: Explicitly recognized as an approved Volume Control BMP for capturing rainfall through soil and vegetation.

Stormwater Detention (Rate Control)

+12

Goal

Slow the release of remaining runoff to prevent downstream sewer surcharging and flooding.

  • Design Storm: Storage and outlet control sized for the 100-year storm event.
  • Standard release rates:
    • ≤ 0.5 acres: Max release of 0.15 cfs (typical 3-inch vortex restrictor).
    • 0.5–1.75 acres: Release rate interpolated between 0.15–0.25 cfs.
  • Lot-to-lot buildings: Where structures occupy > 85% of the lot, storage for rooftop building areas may be based on a 10-year storm, provided 100-year overflows are safely routed.

Purple-Roof® System Fit

Hybrid Green + Blue Roof

Purple-Roof® is a hybrid assembly that pairs green roof retention with controlled blue roof detention. It is designed to meet Chicago's volume and rate requirements without relying on deep rooftop ponding or large underground tanks.

Volume Control

The vegetated layer and needled mineral wool (NMW) retain a significant portion of the 0.5-inch volume requirement for on-site evapotranspiration.

For design, use city-approved retention credits (e.g., 20% soil, 40% NMW under ≥ 4" soil) and check that the combined assembly meets or exceeds the required storage.

Rate Control

The internal honeycomb reservoir and detention layers meter flow laterally to drains, helping limit discharge to the 0.15–0.25 cfs range without exposed rooftop ponding.

Outlet assemblies can be tuned to match the city's required release rate while maintaining positive drainage across the deck.

Operational Benefits
  • +2Cold-weather performance via a ventilated honeycomb layer that maintains drainage in freeze–thaw conditions.
  • +2Reduced reliance on underground detention, freeing at-grade area for parking or amenities.

The system keeps water management at the roof, simplifying plumbing and reducing excavation risk.

Quick Talking Points

  • For regulated Chicago projects, expect to design for both 0.5-inch capture and a 100-year detention event.
  • Purple-Roof® can satisfy both requirements using rooftop systems instead of large underground tanks.
  • Green roof volume counts toward the 0.5-inch requirement; the Purple-Roof® honeycomb controls the release rate.
  • Where buildings cover > 85% of the site, rooftop-focused storage is often the most practical way to comply.
