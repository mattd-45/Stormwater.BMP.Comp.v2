Summary: NYC Stormwater Compliance
for Purple-Roof®

This guide summarizes the NYC Unified Stormwater Rule (USWR) and how Purple-Roof® can support compliance for covered development projects.

1. Regulatory Scope: Covered Development Projects

NYC’s USWR generally applies to projects that meet one or more of the following triggers:

  • Disturb ≥ 20,000 sq. ft. of soil.
  • Create ≥ 5,000 sq. ft. of new impervious surface.
  • Are subject to Local Law 92/94 (Sustainable Roofing Zone: solar or green roof).

The USWR emphasizes a retention-first hierarchy: Retention Detention / Rate Control

2. Performance Standards (High-Level)

NYC uses a tiered hierarchy that prioritizes retention (infiltration and evapotranspiration) before detention. Final criteria vary by drainage area and project context; confirm requirements in DEP’s published guidance.

Water Quality / Runoff Reduction
  • Water Quality Volume: retain or treat runoff from a 1.5-inch rainfall event.
  • Runoff Reduction: high-priority retention; Purple-Roof’s soil + NMW support evapotranspiration pathways.
Sewer Operations / Peak Flow
  • Sewer Operations Volume (CSS areas): manage runoff from a 1.85-inch storm event.
  • Peak flow: release rates are formula-based and may use a weighted coefficient as low as 0.10.

4. NYC Value Proposition

  • LL92/94 synergy: Can satisfy sustainable roofing zone requirements while supporting stormwater compliance in the same footprint.
  • Tax abatement: Priority areas may be eligible for up to $15/sq. ft. in green roof tax abatements (confirm eligibility and current program status).
  • Space salvage: Rooftop-based management can reduce or eliminate underground detention needs for zero-lot-line urban infill projects.
