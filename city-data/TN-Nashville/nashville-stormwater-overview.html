Nashville Stormwater Regulations
& Green Roof Incentives

Summary of Metro Water Services (MWS) stormwater requirements and how green roofs can support compliance and add value.

1. Performance Standards for Regulated Developments

Nashville’s stormwater management is governed by the Metro Water Services (MWS) Stormwater Management Manual, with a strong emphasis on Low Impact Development (LID) to capture, infiltrate, and evapotranspire runoff.

Regulations generally apply to any project requiring a Grading Permit, typically:

  • Sites disturbing ≥ 10,000 sq. ft.; or
  • Sites that are part of a larger common plan of development.
  • Runoff Reduction Volume (RRv): Capture and manage the first 1.1 inches of rainfall using LID practices to the “maximum extent practicable.”
  • Water Quality (80% TSS Removal): For areas where the 1.1‑inch RRv cannot be fully treated through LID, systems must be designed to remove 80% of Total Suspended Solids (TSS) from the remaining Water Quality Volume.
  • Peak Flow Control (Detention): Post‑development peak discharge rates may not exceed pre‑development rates for the 2, 10, 25, 50, and 100‑year, 24‑hour storm events.
  • LID Waiver: If site limitations (e.g., high groundwater, contaminated soils, or tight urban/zero‑lot‑line conditions) prevent achieving the 1.1‑inch RRv, an LID Waiver Application must be submitted to Metro Water Services.

2. Financial & Regulatory Incentives

MWS and local partners provide incentives that make rooftop stormwater controls attractive in dense urban areas.

  • Green Roof Rebate: Rebate of up to $10 per square foot for green roof installation, helping offset the capital cost of moving stormwater management from ground to roof.
  • Stormwater User Fee Credit: Property owners can receive a recurring credit on their Stormwater User Fee (often up to an 80% reduction) for approved and maintained Stormwater Control Measures (SCMs), including green roofs.
  • LID Compliance Path: Green roofs are high‑performance LID practices and are frequently the primary tool for zero‑lot‑line and infill developments to satisfy the 1.1‑inch RRv without waivers or off‑site mitigation.

3. Design Requirements for Green Roofs

To qualify for credit under the MWS Stormwater Management Manual, green roofs must meet specific technical and maintenance criteria.

  • Approved SCM: Green roofs are treated as a Structural Stormwater Control Measure in the Manual.
  • Media & Drainage: Assemblies must include an engineered media layer and underlying drainage/detention layers that together provide both volume retention and peak flow attenuation.
  • Vegetation: Systems must be planted with permanent, drought‑tolerant species (often sedums or native perennials) suited to Nashville’s humid subtropical climate and frequent freeze‑thaw cycles.
  • Maintenance Agreement: A standard Stormwater Control Measure Maintenance Document (see Manual Appendix F) must be executed and recorded, committing the owner to regular inspection and annual maintenance.

4. Developer Value

  • Site Maximization: In dense districts like the Gulch or Broadway, rooftop stormwater management can remove the need for underground vaults, freeing space for additional parking or basement levels.
  • TSS Compliance: Green roofs inherently filter pollutants and can help satisfy the city’s 80% TSS removal requirement for the area they cover.
