Philadelphia Stormwater Regulations
& Green Roof Incentives

Summary of Philadelphia Water Department (PWD) stormwater requirements and how green roofs can be used for compliance and incentives.

1. Performance Standards for Regulated Developments

PWD regulates stormwater through a retention-first, green infrastructure (GI) approach based on the Stormwater Management Guidance Manual (Version 3.1).

Regulations are typically triggered for:

  • Projects disturbing ≥ 15,000 sq. ft. of earth; or
  • Projects in the Combined Sewer System (CSS) area creating ≥ 5,000 sq. ft. of new impervious area.
  • Water Quality (primary requirement): Retain (infiltrate, evaporate, or reuse) the first 1.5 inches of runoff from all Directly Connected Impervious Areas (DCIA).
  • Channel Protection: Post-development peak discharge from the 1-year, 24-hour storm must not exceed the pre-development rate.
  • Flood Control: Peak discharge rates for the 10-year and 100-year storms must not exceed pre-development levels.
  • The 20% Rule (Redevelopment): Redevelopment projects that reduce total impervious cover by 20% or more versus existing conditions can often simplify compliance, and in many cases reduce or bypass full Flood Control and Channel Protection requirements.

2. Financial & Regulatory Incentives

Philadelphia offers strong incentive structures to encourage green roofs and other GI strategies.

  • Green Roof Tax Credit: Business tax credit for 50% of the cost of a green roof, up to $100,000. The green roof must typically cover at least 60% of the total rooftop area.
  • Expedited “Green Review”: Projects that manage 95% or more of post-construction impervious area with approved GI may qualify for an expedited 5‑day plan review.
  • Zoning & Density Bonuses: Under the Philadelphia Code, qualifying green roofs can unlock Floor Area Ratio (FAR) bonuses or residential density adjustments.
  • Parking Landscaping Credits: Interior parking lot landscaping requirements can be reduced on a square‑foot‑for‑square‑foot basis by the area of green roof installed.

3. Design Requirements for Green Roofs

To receive PWD credit, vegetated systems must satisfy specific design criteria.

  • Media Depth: Minimum of 3 inches of engineered growing media for standard green roof credit.
  • Runoff Coefficients (“C” Values): Green roofs are assigned significantly lower runoff coefficients (typically 0.10–0.30) than conventional impervious roofs (around 0.95), which directly increases retention credit.
  • Vegetation: Systems must support permanent, self‑sustaining vegetation. Extensive sedum and succulent mixes are commonly used to meet stormwater mandates.
  • Disconnection: Green roofs are an approved method to “disconnect” rooftop drainage from the sewer system, effectively removing that rooftop area from the on‑site runoff calculation for the remaining site.

4. Compliance Timeline & Process Notes

  • Conceptual Review: Strongly recommended early in design to confirm GI feasibility, roof loading, and routing.
  • Formal Submission: Stormwater Management Plans are submitted and reviewed against the current PWD Guidance Manual criteria.
  • Post‑Construction: Long‑term performance is supported through a recorded maintenance agreement and an Operations & Maintenance (O&M) plan.
